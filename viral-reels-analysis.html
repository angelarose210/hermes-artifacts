The 8 Hook Rules BOTH FORMATS

  1. Always include a skill-level number (3.5, 4.0, 4.5) — the #1 infographic engagement signal
  2. Name the enemy — "bangers", "pushers", "lobbers", or a specific frustration
  3. Use power words: LETHAL, DEADLY, CRUSH, FORCE, DESTROY, ILLEGAL, DISRESPECTFUL, BANNED
  4. Frame as forbidden/secret: "blueprint", "trap", "weapon", "cheat code", "the rule"
  5. Never use generic verbs — "How to improve" = death. "MASTER the LETHAL return" = viral
  6. Negative framing outperforms positive — "STOP making this mistake" > "Learn the right way"
  7. Question format for tutorials — "IS THIS THE MOST [extreme word] [technique]?"
  8. For rule/news hooks: Always add "?" — implies you'll get the answer if you watch

2 Tutorial Editing Patterns

Structure, text systems, trajectory lines, and visual mode switching from our top performers.

Tutorial Video Structure (30–45 seconds)

0:00 – 0:03
THE HOOK — Pattern interrupt visual (3D animation or contrasting B-roll). Bold text overlay (red/neon, distressed font). Music starts immediately.
0:03 – 0:15
THE PROBLEM — Cut to coach/speaker (medium shot). Demonstrate the WRONG technique (exaggerated). Kinetic captions word-by-word (key words in RED).
0:15 – 0:30
THE SOLUTION — Visual mode switch (close-up feet/hands OR wide court shot). Trajectory lines/arrows animate over footage. Slow-motion "proof shot."
0:30 – 0:40
THE BENEFIT / RECAP — Return to medium shot. Confident closing statement. Logo/brand anchor.

Caption System

  • Word-by-word or phrase-by-phrase kinetic captions (Hormozi-style)
  • Base color: WHITE  |  Highlight: RED for key concepts  |  CYAN for technique names
  • Animation: "Pop-in" with slight bounce (overshoot easing)
  • Position: Bottom-third during demos, top-third during hooks
  • Font: Bold condensed sans-serif (Impact / Bebas Neue style), slightly distressed texture

Trajectory Lines THE MONEY SHOT — present in ALL 265K+ videos

  • Style: Glowing red or white line with slight outer glow
  • Animation: "Draw-on" effect (stroke-dasharray 0% → 100%)
  • Timing: Draw over 0.8–1.2s, synced to paddle contact sound
  • Thickness: 4–6px with 8px glow radius
  • Color: RED for power shots  |  CYAN for placement  |  GREEN for legal moves
  • Add animated ball traveling along the path after line draws

3D Wireframe / Anatomy Figure

  • Style: Glowing skeleton/muscular figure on black background
  • Usage: Opening 1–2 seconds as pattern interrupt
  • Color: Neon pink/magenta wireframe lines
  • Animation: Subtle rotation or "breathing" pulse
  • Purpose: Makes content look "gamified" — like a video game special move

Split-Screen Comparisons

  • Layout: 50/50 vertical split
  • Labels: "WRONG" (red) left  |  "RIGHT" (green) right
  • Alt: "Before" vs "After" with colored borders
  • Transition: Wipe from left to right, synced to audio beat

Visual Mode Switching CRITICAL — viral videos switch 5–7 modes per 30s

The 7 visual modes:

  1. 3D animation / wireframe
  2. Talking head (medium shot)
  3. Wide court demonstration
  4. Close-up (hands / feet / paddle)
  5. Slow-motion replay
  6. Tactical top-down diagram
  7. Text / rule citation overlay

Audio-Visual Sync Rules

  • Text pops appear exactly on vocal stress points
  • Trajectory lines draw on paddle-contact "thwack" sound
  • Music beat drops align with visual transitions
  • Jump cuts happen on downbeats

Animation Techniques Cheat Sheet

Stroke / Path Animations

  • SVG stroke-dasharray + stroke-dashoffset
  • Duration: 0.6–1.0s per line
  • Easing: ease-out (fast start, gentle stop)
  • Direction: always in direction of ball travel

Text Animations

  • Headlines: scale-bounce (0.8→1.05→1.0) elastic
  • Section headers: slide-in from right, 0.3s
  • Body: fade-in with 0.2s delay after header
  • Key words: color flash (white→accent→white) 0.4s

Glow / Pulse Effects

  • Neon dividers: opacity 0.7→1.0→0.7 over 2s loop
  • Target circles: scale pulse 1.0→1.05→1.0 every 1.5s
  • Court lines: brightness pulse synced to music

Background

  • Never pure static: slow Ken Burns zoom (102%→100% over 5s)
  • OR: subtle noise/grain overlay (opacity 3–5%)
  • Optional: very slow parallax on separate layers

4 Anti-Patterns — What Kills Videos

From our flat-performing video analysis. Avoid these at all costs.

🚫 Visual Anti-Patterns

  • Static single-camera angle with no cuts
  • No graphics/overlays — raw footage looks amateur
  • No humans visible (object-only shots = zero engagement)
  • Overcast/gray lighting (dull on phone screens)
  • Cluttered backgrounds competing with subject

🔇 Audio Anti-Patterns

  • High-energy music + low-energy visuals = awkward disconnect
  • No voiceover/instruction = no reason to keep watching
  • Generic royalty-free music that doesn't match energy

⏱ Pacing Anti-Patterns

  • Nothing new after second 2 = immediate scroll-away
  • Repetitive visuals (same frame repeating)
  • No "pattern interrupts" to regain wandering attention
  • Duration >60s without visual mode switches

🎣 Hook Anti-Patterns

  • Cliché/generic text ("Only way you'll get me to leave…")
  • No curiosity gap or payoff promise
  • No specific audience targeting (no skill level, no enemy)

6 Production Recipes

Plug-and-play recipes for our three highest-performing content types.

Recipe 1: "The Forbidden Weapon Tutorial"

🎯 200K – 500K
  • 1× controversial/provocative hook claim
  • 1× 3D wireframe animation (2–3 seconds)
  • 3–5 camera angles of technique demonstration
  • 1× trajectory line overlay on ball flight
  • 1× slow-motion proof shot
  • Kinetic captions (word-by-word, key words in RED)
  • Trap/electronic music bed
  • Exaggerated paddle "thwack" sound effects
Timeline: 30–40 seconds Post: Tuesday or Friday, evening UTC

Recipe 2: "The Cheat Sheet Infographic"

🎯 140K – 245K
  • 1× skill-level targeted hook ("THE 4.0 [TECHNIQUE]")
  • Black background with neon accents (cyan + orange)
  • 3–5 tips in grid/stack layout
  • Court diagrams with trajectory arrows
  • White player silhouettes
  • Sequential reveal animation (5 seconds)
Timeline: 5 seconds (forces loops) Post: Any day, evening UTC Batch: Design template once, swap content per topic

Recipe 3: "The Pain Point Fix"

🎯 150K – 180K
  • 1× "WHY YOU [common failure]" hook (red text)
  • B-roll opener (someone else demonstrating the problem)
  • Hard cut to coach explaining
  • "Wrong vs Right" visual comparison
  • Close-up of the specific fix (hands/feet)
  • Benefit statement as closer
Timeline: 35–42 seconds Post: Tuesday or Friday, evening UTC
