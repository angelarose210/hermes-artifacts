Do NOT retire your biggest videos on low percentages ›

Low percentages on a high-reach video = success, not failure

A naive "low CTR + low AVP = retire" read would throw out 15 Tips Part 1 (25.7K views, 558 subs, your #1 video) and Part 2 (252 subs). That would be a mistake.

When YouTube pushes a video wide into browse/suggested, it reaches a colder, broader audience — both CTR and AVP fall by definition. The quadrant tool only applies to mid-reach videos. Breakouts are judged on absolute views + subs, full stop.

The rules

Lead with a number or year · use one power/curiosity word (NASTY, deadly, WEAPON, secret all over-index) · frame as "for YOU" not "here's a topic" · never put jargon (nvz, dink terminology) in the title — say it plainly · put the exact model name in review titles, people search it.

🎓 Playbook A — Instructional

Job: grow the subscriber base (avg 82 subs/video, ~3x paddle). The discovery + authority engine — every sub becomes a future paddle buyer, so this funnel feeds Playbook B.

Failure point: the 0:17–0:22 spoken intro

"Hey it's Jeff, in this video we'll cover…" — 15 Tips Part 2 drops −11.9 pts in 5 seconds right there. Multi-payoff listicles hold; single-skill linear tutorials (topspin dinks 44%, volley shots 31%) are the lowest retainers.

🎬 Jeff Filming / on-camera ›
  • Script a cold open, not an intro. First words = tip #1. No "welcome," no "in this video."
  • Demo first, explain over the replay. Every rewind spike on the channel is on a demonstration, never on talking.
  • Structure everything as a numbered list — even a single technique becomes "3 keys to the topspin dink."
  • Open sequels cold on new value — zero recap. Part 2 underperformed Part 1 partly on a recap intro.
✂️ Editor Editing / packaging ›
  • Cut the spoken intro entirely. Branding → 1–2s lower-third, never spoken. Fixes the −12pt cliff on nearly every video.
  • Hard-cut tip-to-tip with an on-screen chapter card. Kill "so that was tip one…" Fixes the 0:45–0:51 cliff.
  • Add chapters to every video ≥4 min. Currently zero have them — unlocks Key Moments + ~4% AVD. Biggest quick win.
  • Keep the 9-minute flagship long. Low AVP% but holds each viewer 257s, drives the most subs + ad revenue. Fix its intro, keep its body.
  • Double down on numbered franchises — "15 Tips" is your #1 video ever. Build a recurring series.

🏓 Playbook B — Paddle / Gear

Job: affiliate conversion — Friday especially. Ad revenue here is tiny ($350 lifetime); the real money is affiliate sales YouTube can't see. These videos monetize the audience instructional content built.

⚠️ The trust correction — read this first

Flashing a discount code early makes a review feel like a sales pitch, and that kills conversion harder than a slow open. The fix is NOT "code early." It's "decision early."

Your best paddle video (Aura Pro, 65% retention, 10.6% CTR) opened as a buying guide — "which shape is right for YOU." Your worst (JOOLA, 44%) led with specs.

The sequence that earns the sale: open on honest decision framing → deliver a credible, useful review → let the code land at the verdict rewind-spike, where the viewer has just decided "I want that paddle." There, the code is the answer to a question they're already asking — not an interruption.

🎬 Jeff Filming / on-camera ›
  • Open on the decision, not the specs. "Here's who this paddle is for / who should skip it." That's the 65% template vs the 44% one.
  • Be honestly critical. Credibility is what converts. An honest "this isn't for everyone" earns the buy; pure praise reads as paid.
  • Save the best demo for the verdict moment — where people rewind, and where the code naturally belongs.
✂️ Editor Editing / packaging ›
  • Cold-open on the verdict/decision framing. Move the verdict rewind-spike near the front; specs to the back for buyers who stay.
  • Code at the verdict beat, on screen, held there — the natural conversion moment, not the cold open. Doubles as FTC disclosure.
  • Title/thumbnail = "which paddle for YOU" + the exact model name (people search it).
  • Fix descriptions that open with an affiliate link — it eats the search preview. Hook first, link below the fold.
  • Clip the verdict + demo as Shorts to feed the review.
⏱️ Editor Timing — owns the calendar ›
  • Drop a paddle review when both are true: (a) Friday has something launching, and (b) you've just had an instructional video pop. Convert a fresh, warm subscriber wave — not a cold audience.
