🎓 Playbook A — Instructional Videos

GROW SUBS

Job: grow the subscriber base. This is the engine. 15 Tips Part 1 alone drove 558 subscribers. Every sub here is a future paddle buyer.

The failure point: the 0:17–0:22 spoken intro. "Hey it's Jeff, in this video we'll cover…" is where people leave.

The pattern the data proves: multi-payoff beats single-skill, decisively. 4 Spin Serves (4 discrete payoffs, 60.8% AVP) and 15 Tips (listicle) hold strong. Topspin Dinks (one skill, slow linear build, 43.9%) is the worst instructional video. Each payoff beat pulls the viewer to the next.

🎬 Jeff FILMING / ON-CAMERA
  • Script a cold open, not an intro. Your first words = tip #1 or the first instruction. No "welcome," no "in this video."
  • Demo first, explain over the replay. Every rewind spike is on a demonstration, never on talking. Hit the shot, then narrate it.
  • Structure everything as a numbered list — even a single technique becomes "3 keys to the topspin dink." Gives the viewer a reason to stay for the next number.
  • Open sequels cold on new value — zero recap. Part 2 underperformed Part 1 on every metric, and its worst cliff is the recap zone.
✂️ Editor EDITING / PACKAGING
  • Cut the spoken intro entirely. Branding becomes a 1–2 second lower-third, never spoken. (This fixes the −12-point cliff that's on every video.)
  • Hard-cut tip-to-tip with an on-screen chapter card. Kill every "so that was tip one…" (Fixes the 0:45–0:51 cliff.)
  • Don't shorten the 9-minute flagship to chase a prettier percentage. It holds each viewer 257 seconds — longer than the entire runtime of your best short video — and drives the most subs. Fix its intro, keep its dense body.
  • Add chapters to every video ≥4 min. Unlocks Google Key Moments, adds ~4% AVD, lets viewers skip the bleed zone. Currently zero long-form videos have them — biggest quick win on the channel.
  • Double down on numbered franchises. Extend "15 Tips" into a recurring series ("X mistakes over-50 players make," "X serve-return rules").

🏓 Playbook B — Paddle / Gear Videos

REVENUE

Job: affiliate conversion — Friday especially, which converts well. These barely move subscribers (3–17 each) and that's fine. They monetize the audience the instructional videos built.

The failure point is earlier and more brutal: 0:03–0:19. Reviews open on spec recitation, so people leave before the verdict. That's the most expensive bleed on the channel, because this is where the real revenue is.

⚠️ The trust correction — read this first

Flashing a discount code early makes a review feel like a sales pitch, and that kills conversion harder than a slow open does. The fix is NOT "code early." It's "decision early."

Your best paddle video (Friday Aura Pro shapes, 65% AVP) opened as a buying guide — "which shape is right for YOU" — not a pitch and not a spec dump. Your worst (JOOLA, 44%) led with specs.

The sequence that earns the sale: open on the honest decision framing → deliver a credible, genuinely useful review → let the code land at the natural conversion moment (the verdict rewind-spike), where the viewer has just decided "I want that paddle." At that point the code is the answer to a question they're already asking, not an interruption. Disclosure ("Friday sponsors me — here's my honest take") up front actually builds credibility instead of undercutting it.

🎬 Jeff FILMING / ON-CAMERA
  • Open on the decision, not the specs. Lead with "here's who this paddle is for / who should skip it." That's the 65% template vs the 44% one.
  • Be honestly critical. The credibility of the verdict is what converts. A review that only praises reads as paid; an honest "this isn't for everyone" earns the buy.
  • Save the most useful demo for the verdict moment — that's where people rewind, and where the code naturally belongs.
✂️ Editor EDITING / PACKAGING
  • Cold-open on the verdict/decision framing. The 4:31 rewind spike on Friday vs Aura is the verdict — move it near the front. Specs go to the back for the buyers who stayed.
  • Place the code at the verdict beat, on screen, held there — the natural conversion moment, not the cold open. Doubles as clean FTC disclosure real estate.
  • Frame the title + thumbnail as "which paddle for YOU," not "specs." Lead with the model name (people search exact paddle names).
  • Fix existing descriptions that open with an affiliate link — it eats the search preview. Hook first, link below the fold.
  • Clip the verdict + demo as Shorts to feed the review (and these are where your big-view Shorts come from anyway).
⏱️ Timing EDITOR OWNS THE CALENDAR
  • Drop a paddle review when both are true: (a) Friday has something launching, and (b) you've just had an instructional video pop — you're converting a fresh, warm subscriber wave instead of a cold audience.
