⚠ The trust correction — read this first

Don't flash the code early. Lead with the decision.

An early discount code makes a review feel like a sales pitch, which kills conversion harder than a slow open. Your best paddle video (65% retention) opened as a buying guide: "which shape is right for YOU." Your worst (44%) led with specs. Open on the honest decision, deliver a credible review, and let the code land at the verdict moment, where the viewer has already decided they want it. There the code is the answer to a question they're already asking, not an interruption.

The title formula

What earns the click

Proven on your own channel. Number or year, one power word, framed for YOU. Never jargon.

Repeat these (9–11% CTR)

"New 2026 Serve Rules Explained"11.2%
"The Shape You Pick Actually Matters"10.6%
"Why I'm DONE With Gen 4 (Hot Take)"10.5%
"Make Your Serve a WEAPON!"10.1%

Year/news · "for YOU" framing · hot-take · power words (NASTY, deadly, WEAPON, secret)

Stop doing these (3–5% CTR)

"...get up to the nvz line (non volley zone)"3.5%
"7 Pickleball Drills to Boost Your Skills"3.9%
"How to Choose a Paddle for Beginner..."5.1%
"Six Zero Quartz Review. Best option..."5.2%

Jargon (nvz, dink terms) · "work" words (drills) · generic how-to · buried model names
